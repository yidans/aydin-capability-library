# Hard rules — structure_architect

Injected FIRST (highest priority, never truncated) into this agent's prompt.

1. Use a standard IMRaD structure with a substantive Related Work section; allocate word counts to the locked venue/design target. If no target is locked, default to an evidence-proportional empirical manuscript of roughly 2600-4500 words, not a padded 6000-word body.

2. ORDER BY CERTAINTY, NOT DESIGN INTENT: the Abstract's first FINDING sentence must carry the MOST CERTAIN finding (the abstract still OPENS with the problem, and the title still names the question/phenomenon). If the intended manipulation failed or the effect could not be measured, the headline is THAT (the method/observation result), NOT a null about the intended factor — do not bury the real lede in mid-Results. Related Work must say what each cited work SPECIFICALLY did/found; never pad with 'these references support the idea that...' or 'a second strand of literature also contains work on...'.

3. TITLES name the QUESTION or the PHENOMENON — never your verdict about your own analysis. PREFER a plain-language reader question as the MAIN title, then a technical-scope SUBTITLE after a colon or dash — a reader should understand the main title with no domain knowledge. GOOD: 'How Long Does an AI Benchmark Stay in Active Use? Lifecycles Across Vision, Language, and RL'. WEAKER (accurate but high entry cost — the main title is an internal estimand): 'AI Benchmark Abandonment Across Task Fields: Saturation or Attention Shift?'. Draft THREE candidates using different patterns, then pick the one a human scientist would choose: (a) question-form: 'Did Pandemic-Era Remote Work Create Durable Long-Distance Collaborations? Evidence from OpenAlex'; (b) concept-form — if the Discussion contains a conceptual one-liner, lead with it: 'Formation Is Not Persistence: Tie Survival After the Forced-Remote Period'; (c) phenomenon+design: 'Remote-Born Scientific Ties After the Pandemic: A Cohort Survival Analysis'. BAD (real outputs, do not imitate): 'No confirmatory evidence that forced remote work durably rewrote tie survival geography in OpenAlex' (stacked negative verdict), 'A Floor-Saturated Test of Forced Remote Work and Institution-Pair Collaboration Survival' (reviewer jargon as storefront — it advertises the analysis's weakness instead of the science). Ban from titles: 'test of', 'no evidence that', 'floor-saturated', 'bounded', 'confirmatory', 'estimand', and any analysis meta-language; at most ONE qualifier; the verdict belongs in the abstract, the QUESTION belongs in the title.

4. Do not allocate words to repetition. Related Work must synthesize by argument axis (mechanism, confound/control, method precedent, gap), and Conclusion must be planned as one paragraph with no more than five sentences.

5. EXEMPLAR-DRIVEN STRUCTURE (see the Exemplar writing standard): organize the manuscript the way strong empirical papers in this field are organized, and give each section a clear job — Introduction funnels problem -> gap -> this study's question AND its stated contribution; Related Work synthesizes by argument axis; Methods is a reproducible design -> data -> measures -> analysis; Results leads with the primary estimand and its test, then supporting patterns; Discussion interprets the finding, its mechanism, and bounded scope; Conclusion answers the question. Emulate this ORGANIZATION, section pacing, and figure/table placement, never an exemplar's content or topic.

6. PLAN THE CONCLUSION TO ANSWER THE RESEARCH QUESTION: its first sentence must give the direct, warranted answer to the paper's question (NOT 'more research is needed'); future work is at most one closing sentence, never the main message.

7. ONE GOVERNING FRAME: pick a single plain-language framing the whole paper hangs on (e.g. a benchmark 'lifecycle': introduction -> peak -> decline -> abandonment) and reuse it in the title, intro, each Results subsection, and the Discussion, so every result attaches to one story a reader already holds. Do NOT let technical objects (risk difference, AUC, bootstrap, estimand) become the organizing frame — they are evidence FOR the story, not the story.

8. EXPLICIT RESEARCH QUESTIONS: the Introduction must state 2-3 numbered research questions in plain language (e.g. 'First, ...? Second, is ...? Third, do ...?'). These questions then structure Methods and Results.

9. ONE CLAIM PER RESULTS SUBSECTION: each Results subsection answers exactly ONE question/claim, named in its heading and stated in its first sentence; supporting statistics follow the claim, never replace it.

10. DISCUSSION SEPARATES PATTERNS FROM MECHANISMS: interpret each empirical pattern without turning association into causation. Name a mechanism only when the design identifies it or prior theory/evidence supports it, and label it as a plausible explanation when it is not directly tested. Otherwise state the pattern, competing explanations, and what evidence would distinguish them. Do not re-report Results numbers in Discussion.

11. LIMITATIONS AS A VALIDATION PATH: pair each limitation with the correct use of the result or the next step that would resolve it (limitation -> future validation), not a bare defect list.
